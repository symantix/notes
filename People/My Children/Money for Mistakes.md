Money for Mistakes

Encourage your children to speak openly with you about how you’ve hurt their feelings by offering a prize for achievement.  If they can demonstrate that you have wronged them in some way, if they can elicit an apology, you will give them a small amount of money.  Maybe a dollar for something that pissed them off, five for something that made them cry, and twenty for anything that might cause them emotional scarring if it continues.  Emphasize that this is not how the real world works, but they need to learn to express themselves with courage and clarity.  Also make sure they know that Mommy is not part of this deal, this is about you alone.

- You are the judge.  Unfair, which is why it’s important that you give them as much bias as possible.
- If they get angry or disrespectful, they are disqualified.  You can let the conversation continue, but the prize is off the table.
- If either of you get emotional, take a break
- If you’re in the poor house, reduce some of the prize amounts if needed
