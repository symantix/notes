I regret any misunderstandings that you and I have had in our past.  

I use that term broadly, to include anything that drove us apart, whether deliberate or accidental, on either of our parts.  I have tried to write this letter to you a thousand times.  Of course, I regret most of all that we drifted apart, and I know that I played no small part in that.  But in that drifting apart, the biggest thing we missed, in my opinion, was each other’s stories.  We didn’t know each other’s stories, and we were barely a part of them.  And so all that was left, was to share them.

And this brings me to my point.  In my world, there has been so much that I have never been able to share with you, so many stories from my young adult life that you may never know, because so much of it revolves around a man that I still don’t believe you want to hear anything about.  These stories are not just about him, but about all the people in Jasper, and the people here, and the adventures we had, some for better, some for worse.  I regret now that part of my journey had to involve plunging deeper into superstition than I had ever been before.  But that was the culture there in East Texas, and I was young then.  The story has a beautiful ending, which includes me taking that man off of the pedestal that he never asked me to put him on.  And though I still sometimes give of myself until it hurts, I do so now with my eyes wide open, to a community that has followed me on this journey, and to a pastor who has proven himself to be a worthy steward.  We were never a commune, but we certainly were a church.  And now we are, at the very least, a community.

None of this ever made you less special.  But I always wished you could’ve seen it all through my eyes.  Maybe you would have understood better.  

It’s like that one time, when I told you that God hated Esau.  That was so early on, and I was repeating what the people around me were saying.  I presumed it was what our pastor believed, because I had heard him mention the verse.  What I didn’t understand at that time, is that he is a textual critic.  He never taught that god hated anyone, quite the opposite as time would bear out.  

And that is certainly a big reason why he has stood the test of time in my life.  As I began to slowly wake up from the religious spells of that region, as I began to ask questions and argue with him, he usually didn’t fight back.  I would finish most conversations with a shocking realization of how much my own beliefs, even those from my mild childhood churching, had caused me to hear him through a filter that he never intended.  It took me a while to see that the way he was teaching, his critical approach to the bible, had always been designed to wake people up, usually at their own pace.  

And that’s also why there are so many others in that region, whom I’m simply no longer connected to.  They were only willing to step so far away from there superstitions and prejudices, until they were done with him, with NCG, and so certainly done with me.

I’m sorry that I once wanted to get you saved.  As for now, as for what is left, I have had only two wishes to bring this matter to a close, and the first one has been fulfilled, and is being fulfilled, and will continue to be fulfilled.  And that wish is that you would see in my being, the best of yourself, the best of those we loved together, and finally, the best of a father that you never knew.  And that perhaps this would, in some small way, open your mind to some new interpretations of our fragmented past.  

As for the second wish.  I had once resigned, once accepted that it would never happen.  And I remain in that place.  Even to send this letter to you now feels edgy.  Nevertheless, one of my greatest wishes is to one day have another chance to spend some time with you, a lot of time, in quality grown-up conversation, and to share some of these stories with you, without feeling like you may still see me as a religious zealot, for which there is no doubt that I once was.  And if, at the end of the day, it turns out that I still am, I have always appreciated your critical ear.
