Dreams are often delayed, often frustrated by reality and responsibility.  We think of a crazy idea, but we know it’s actually a great idea, and we think some more about it, and hope, and maybe, shockingly, we might even try.  Often someone taps us on the shoulder.  Maybe a friend, maybe just our own conscience.  It tells us that there are more pressing matters, and our time and resources are needed elsewhere.  We can keep move forward toward the dream if we wish, but we know that others will be hurt, or our life will be thrown off balance.  So we come back to earth, and we are reminded, once again, of our limitations.

But that doesn’t mean that we shouldn’t dream.  It doesn’t even mean that we shouldn’t have tried.  If we think we have failed, then we have failed to understand life.  Things start, and then they stop, and then they start again.  So dream.  And when it’s time to stop, stop.  And when the moment comes back again, and it always does, you must decide whether to continue your dream, or start a new one.  After all, you can always come back to the other dream later.
