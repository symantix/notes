Lately, I’ve been watching a lot of UFC and playing video games at the same time.  I don’t always care about the fight, but I like the noise, so I play my games and tune the fight in and out as needed.  I don’t really care much about the fears that screens are taking over our lives.  I like a lot of screens, and a lot of noise.  

I grew up in an open floor plan elementary school.  That meant that you could hear the noise of every class, all 30 of them across six grades, rumble and reverberate across the central library into the spaces of every other class.  It was like working and learning in a stadium.  The teachers often insisted on quiet in their class space, but that had no bearing on the class spaces next to us, or those on the other side of the school, across the library.  Sometimes the energy was off the charts.  I don’t know why, but I don’t ever remember feeling like I was in the midst of chaos, and I don’t remember any other kids complaining about it either.  

After that, I went to junior high, and the rooms were closed off, as they were in high school, as they were in any office job.  Distractions were discouraged, even punished.  And now I’ve lived over 20 years of my adult life, and I finally realize, I thrive in the midst of noise.  So all day, I play UFC or sci-fi on a screen in the background, something exciting that is also easy to tune out, because I’ve seen it 20 times before or because I just don’t care that much about it.  And the noise fills the room with other adventures that I’m not a part of, while I work on my computer throughout the day, and play video games in the evenings.  

Today, I was sitting on my porch doing that very thing, enjoying the noise and the entertainment, and somewhere beyond the noise, I heard a mockingbird sing, and I turned off my noise for a moment, and I listened.  And as the mockingbird sang its many songs, the wind began to blow through the trees, and I looked down, and a bumblebee was inspecting the many weeds and wildflowers in my yard, and the trees were swaying, and the sun was shining, and the clouds were rolling.  It was like the voice of God blowing through the universe.  And I remembered why I like to sit on my porch, and surround myself with the sounds and feelings of nature.  And so that day, I turned off the UFC, I turned off the video games, and as I listened to the voice of God, I wrote a song.  

...On a screen.







Screens are windows that bring us close to different realities, many of which we might never experience without screens.  But there’s nothing like the creation that lives and breathes all around us.  That is what makes being stuck behind a screen all day, worth it, and that is why we must all put down our screens on a regular basis.  Think of it as prayer, or meditation, or a reset.
