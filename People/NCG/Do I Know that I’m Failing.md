The question “do I know that I’m failing?”, is a hard question to ask yourself.  You can’t ask that question without battling the implication that you are failing.  But sometimes you need to ask yourself that question, even if you’re not.  When I ask myself “do I know I’m failing?”, I’m asking 1) Am I failing, 2) Do I know about it, and 3) Have I suppressed the information because, for some reason, I still have a strong desire to continue down a failing path.  I often find myself in such a place.  Just like others will hide the truth from me in order to continue down a reckless path, I may also hide the truth from myself.  I may see it in the corner of my eye, just enough to instinctively know that I need to ignore it if I want to continue.  I must watch for those moments.  The truth about ourselves may sometimes be hard to face, but it’s the only path to correction.  Think of correction like correcting a cars direction with the steering wheel.  Correction leads to improvement.  Improvement leads to success.  Success leads to greater possibilities, like faster speed, more gears, or maybe even a little cruise control.  Keep correcting, keep improving, in every part of your life, and you will soon find yourself at many of the most amazing destinations, on the true journey of exploration and discovery.  This is the evolution of humanity.
