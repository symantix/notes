For many of us, church made us better people.  But Jesus was always the placebo.  He was something to talk about amongst people who are all so different, and sometimes have little to nothing in common.  He was the best thing to talk about, a great human, a perfect human, someone we should all be like.  It instilled trust when we saw others we did not know, wanting to be like the perfect person who never hurt anyone.  But it was never him, it was always us.  We were the ones drawing near, gathering together, speaking of idealism, and imagining that we could achieve it, and applying and allying ourselves to the task.  Jesus was an excuse to never close discourse with one another, at least not at the church.  The effect was so powerful that the extremisms of Christianity fanned the flames of war across its entire continent, for centuries, to destroy those who didn’t speak the same religious language that we did.  So it’s time to bring our religious icons down a peg or two, without giving up the true value of gathering together.
