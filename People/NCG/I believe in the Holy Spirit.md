I believe in the Holy Spirit. I believe it’s real.  And when it’s in you, it moves you, and you move others. If you’re trying to move others, your children, your friends, an audience; trying to get them to do something, to change, to work together; if they don’t move, it may be that you need to seek this spirit.  So many preachers think that holiness is equal to piousness or sinlessness.  They stand in front of their people, and they tell them what they’re doing wrong, and what the penalty is if they don’t straighten up.  We parents like to preach too, and when we speak to our children or our people in this way, any of us, we are failing.  This spirit we seek shouldn’t be called holy until we understand the true meaning of the word.  It is the spirit of inspiration, the spirit of the refeshed inner self, the spirit that is as ready for life as we were when we were children, the spirit that draws near to wise people, and flees from foolish behavior, the spirit that encourages but never condemns, the spirit that soothes the beast within us, that delivers us from our fears, the spirit that makes us know that we have the power to master this life, to bring order to chaos, to reach our dreams.  And when it dwells in us, it lives in and around us, and it moves us to do better.
