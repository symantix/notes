It almost seems like, the further we move along in The Bible, the less tainted by man the text is.  And it doesn’t feel like a translation or transmission issue.  It feels more like the evolution of man, as we begin to grasp, more and more, what perfection truly looks like.  At one time, we thought perfection was the greatest warriors, and so God and all of his mortal heroes are creatures of war and wrath.  But slowly we gained the ability to reflect deeply upon ourselves, as individuals, and then more recently as a species.  And little by little, as we watched ourselves destroy each other, we began to see the cracks and and chinks in the warriors perfect armor.  Considering how limited the viewpoint was of almost any human being in his day, it’s astonishing how insightful Jesus’ living commentary was on the state of humanity, and more importantly, the state of divinity.
