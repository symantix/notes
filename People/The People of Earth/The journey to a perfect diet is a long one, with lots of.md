Hi Jim!  I want to tell you, I enjoy watching your journey, and I’m really happy to hear you’re working on your diet.  Improving your diet can help you in many ways, including building resilience for all that outdoor work.

The journey to a perfect diet is a long one, with lots of conflicting information.  Be patient with the process and don’t draw conclusions too quickly.

I know you won’t want to hear this, lol, but I will just tell you about my personal experience.  

I’ve been improving the kind of meat that I’ve been eating for years, in an effort to improve my blood pressure.  This includes learning to smoke meat, and ended with a mostly chicken and salmon diet.  With this and exercise, I saw small improvements, but I failed at reducing my meds.  

Then, two months ago, I dropped all meat and dairy from my diet, and switched to plant-based, and Monica joined me (for the most part).  We decided to treat it as a 1-month experiment. Our diet was mostly fresh vegetables, sautéed in a pan after watching some good online tutorials.  It also included fresh fruit, whole-grain and sourdough bread. When cooking and seasoning our bread, replaced butter with olive oil and salt.

Within two days, most of my life-long allergies vanished and all my life-long stomach issues went away.  On the third day, I accidentally drank some milk, and my nose immediately closed.  That’s when I figured out that much of my allergies were never hay-fever, as I was told, but a reaction to milk.  Within a week, after years of trying and failing, to my astonishment, I reduced my blood pressure meds, while salting my food as much as I wanted.  In less than a month, I dropped most excess fat, and now it’s almost all gone.  Monica is dropping weight and experiencing other benefits as well. Joint pain has lessened, I feel sturdier and more resilient.  There’s zero cholesterol in this diet, so that pill is also gone.

I still eat meat and eggs from time to time, maybe 1-4 meals a month.  And I haven’t concluded that this is the perfect diet.  But this isn’t about animal ethics, or the fact that humans have been carnivores since the dawn of time.  It’s about human health, and what’s best for you.  

There are lots of great tips, recipes, and tutorials online, to keep your stomach full of delicious vegetables and fruit all day long, as well as whole grain and sourdough breads that will knock your socks off (Bailey’s Farmers Market has a decent price on amazing sourdough, but you’ll save money on veggies at Walmart).  And, if you stick to mostly fresh fruits and vegetables, and bread, you’ll likely save some serious money.

Anyway, I hope you find the perfect diet for you. 🍺
