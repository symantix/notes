The biggest problem with fools is the bandwidth they take up in your mind if you engage them, especially compared to the potential cost.  The cost of a mere conversation could be hours of time that you could’ve spent bettering yourself meaningfully.  An ongoing conversation with a lifelong opponent could be devastating to your personal growth.  And of course, engaging with the wrong opponent could cost you your very life.

However, if you look at them as what they truly are, a tool possessed by their ideas, you come closer to their proper usefulness in a reasonable person’s life.  If you are disciplined with your time, if you limit their intrusiveness, they become a method of practice, a technique for improving one’s own ability to think around challenging topics.  You shouldn’t expect them to become anything more than that, so that the likely outcome is not perceived as a loss, and a the unlikely outcome is a surprise worthy of celebration.
