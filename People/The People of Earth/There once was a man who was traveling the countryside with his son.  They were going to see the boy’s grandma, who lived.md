There once was a man who was traveling the countryside with his son.  They were going to see the boy’s grandma, who lived in the mountains.   They had only one donkey, and although it could carry two people, they also had luggage.  So they had to decide who would ride, and who would walk.  After a short discussion, they decided that they would take turns, and the child would go first.

As they moved along, they encountered another group of travelers going in the opposite direction.  Seeing the child riding upon the donkey, and the father walking, they said, “Why aren’t these two wearing masks?  We are in the middle of a pandemic.  They should be ashamed”.

After the two had stopped and rested for a while, the father got on the donkey to take his turn, and the son lead the way on foot.  The words of the other travelers made them worry, so they both wore a mask as they bore the heat of the day.

Later that day, they encountered another group of travelers.  These people wore no masks.  “Look at this ignorant pair”, they said as they passed by.  “Masks do nothing to keep you from getting sick, and yet these two must certainly think they are better than us.”

That night they camped and slept.  In the morning the donkey was still tired, so together the father and the son carried the donkey, and all of the luggage, upon their backs.  They decided that it was impossible to please everyone, so they wore their masks around their necks as they travelled through the heat of the day.  They decided that if they encountered any other travelers, they would simply do as those travelers did, so that there would be no trouble.

Later that day, as the donkey rested comfortably upon the backs of its humans, they did indeed come upon another band of travelers moving in the opposite direction.  The father looked and saw that they all wore masks, so the two of them pulled their masks over their mouth and nose, and prepared to wish the approaching party a good day.  As the other travelers came near, one of them shouted, “Look at these lazy people who hate animals!  Can they not be bothered to put a mask on their donkey as well?!?” 

When they got to Grandma’s house, they knocked upon the door, and prepared for a long-awaited reunion.  But instead, Grandma refused to let them in.  “Go home, you inconsiderate jackasses!” she shouted, “Don’t you know that I’m in the age bracket that is most vulnerable to this virus?  Are you trying to kill me?!?”

So they turned around and went home.  But the donkey was still tired, so he stayed and kept Grandma company.

“And next time call first!” Grandma shouted as they left her property.

The End
