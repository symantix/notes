Most of life‘s memory loss has less to do with age and more to do with slowly realizing how flawed our memory has always been. This realization comes more quickly when we start trying to take responsibility and bring order to our lives and lay out our daily routines and follow through on what we say will do.  As children, many of these things seem less important, and so when we forget, we just blow it off, and turn our attention to the next moment in life, much like an old lady swimming in the gentle pools of dimentia.
