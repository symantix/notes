We once asked, should we tolerate those who are different from us?  Once most of us settled this question, we then asked, should we tolerate everyone?  And then, should we tolerate the intolerant?

And now, should we tolerate those who have no tolerance for us?  What we are really asking is:  How far is too far?

When Jesus said, love your enemy, I believe he was speaking about those who are different from us, and those who are intolerant, and those who are intolerant of the intolerant, and those who are intolerant of us.  I do not believe he was speaking of everyone, except perhaps in the most celestial sense.  But aside from the most egregious criminal offenders, everyone deserves to be treated with the respect of life without any impediment from me.

So, how far is too far?  When Jesus was asked this question, this was His reply:  Forgive your fellow man, seventy times seven, for whatever you perceive to be their offense.  It is this principle that keeps our society from losing its grip on reality, and slipping into mass delusion.
