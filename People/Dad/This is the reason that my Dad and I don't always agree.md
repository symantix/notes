This is the reason that my Dad and I don't always agree.  From day one, I have refused to be a yes man, to simply accept everything he says, as a pastor, teacher, or otherwise.  I have always questioned anything that doesn't make complete sense to me, sometimes, perhaps, to the point of being disruptive.  And yet he has won most arguments.  For this, he deserves my respect.  He has not won all arguments, nor has he fought against my every protest.  He has both lost and relented, sometimes at great cost.  For this, he deserves my trust.  But for the patience that he had with me in the first years of my adult life, and for the transformative power of our conversations and his messages, he deserves my love.
