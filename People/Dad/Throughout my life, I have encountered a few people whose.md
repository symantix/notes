Throughout my life, I have encountered a few people whose presence I enjoyed so terribly, and was hurt to find that they didn't feel the same about me.  Nobody was ever rude enough to say this to my face, but their body language and overall behavior toward me made it clear enough. And although I took it very personally when I was young, I do try harder to be more understanding these days. After all, if I don't enjoy being around someone, I do feel that it's my right to exercise my own will in such matters, and so I must accept that others should have this right as well. 

In recent years, I have slowly become a bit more extroverted, almost naturally so. There is one main reason for this:  For some reason, I am able to read people a bit more clearly these days, and I'm starting to see that there in fact there are many people who enjoy my presence very much.  And since I often keep to myself, I see now that I have indeed made them feel a bit like I did, a bit more alone in the world.  And I observed their body language, and I saw that, in some cases, it was practically identical to the body language that, at one time, I read as rejection of me, not my rejection of them.
