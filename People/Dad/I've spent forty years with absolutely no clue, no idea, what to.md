I've spent forty years with absolutely no clue, no idea, what to do with my most vile thoughts. I guess I just let them sit there, and try to ignore them?  Or should I try to silence them, or push them deep down?  Okay, so I had two ideas.  The point is, nothing worked.  They always persisted, or rose back to the top.

Tonight I had a vile thought cross my mind, and suddenly, without much forethought, I physically turned to that thought, as if it was a man sitting on the couch next to me, and I replied to him.  My reply was in my imagination, but it was loud, clear, and firm.  My reply was not empty, but meaningful.  I don't remember what that thought had to say, and I doubt I would write it down if I did.  I don’t remember what my reply was.  All I remember is that I had moved on moments later, and that the moment had become interesting to me, rather than disturbing.
