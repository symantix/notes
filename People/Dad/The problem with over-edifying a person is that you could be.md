The problem with over-edifying a person is that you could be leading them in a direction that is a complete waste of time for them.  If you praise a person for their wonderful artwork, when you really don’t think it’s that good, they might end up spending thousands of hours developing talent that they just don’t have…(do I really believe this?  This implies that a person can’t get good at something simply by pouring hours and hours of practice into it.  I don’t think I believe that.[^1]

[^1]:The point is that you shouldn’t give someone a false perception of your opinion.  People are trying to measure the world around them.  Let them.  You can be kind, but try to offer them as much constructive honesty as you can.  Leave the decisions concerning dedication, in their hands, and let them make the decision based on the feedback they ask for.  By withholding all manner of critique, you are taking matters that belong to them, into your own hands.)
