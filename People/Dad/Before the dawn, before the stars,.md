  

Before the dawn, before the stars,

Beyond the reach of who we are,

There lies a source, unspoken, high—

Not of this world, nor in the sky.

If God created existence then,

God cannot be found among us existentially.

No measure, logic, sight, or sound

Can circle what’s not earthly bound.

  

  

God is beyond the lines we draw,

Outside the reach of mind and law.

Existence is because He wills—

But cannot grasp what He fulfills.

Truth and meaning, time and space,

Are echoes in His hidden place.

We search with reason, we seek and strive,

But only revelation brings Him alive.

  

  

Our words and thoughts are cast in clay,

Shaped by the world, bound by the day.

Yet what is holy, wholly other,

Can’t be defined by sister or brother.

All axes of our thought will break,

Before the mystery they can’t remake.

Truth is a mirror, dim and brief,

But God’s beyond both joy and grief.

If God is source and we are clay,

We cannot find Him our own way.

No science, language, sense, or art

Can chart the path from end to start.

If knowing comes, it’s a gift, not a prize—

A spark of truth that breaks the skies.

  

Faith receives what thought cannot,

And awe remains where words fall short.

The Incarnate Word, is alive within—

And it reveals the heart of God.
