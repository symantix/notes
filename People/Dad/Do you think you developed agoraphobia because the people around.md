Do you think you developed agoraphobia because the people around you were far more dependent on you than your mind was comfortable with, far more dependent than your mind thought was fair, and since you were not doing anything about it consciously, your body finally decided to activate its own subconscious method of hiding you away in a safe place?  Agoraphobia gives you freedom from the ire of those who are using you, since your withdrawal is technically out of your control, and therefore you are not directly to blame.

Sometimes I wonder if I get a light, moderately controlled version of agoraphobia toward the end of a job that is supposed to be permanent. I take the time to formulate an exit plan, but my desire to exit feels involuntary and irresistible.  Sometimes, particularly with this last job, it has felt like a controlled panick.
