The problem with poverty, imo, is less that people don’t know how to wait for two marshmallows, and more that there are simply never two marshmallows to wait for.  The choice is usually no marshmallows now, unless your family has some extra to spare, and then maybe you’ll get a half marshmallow later.  For me personally, for example, I always find it such a breath of fresh air when I can reach a place where saving money for something, anything, is even feasible.  And when I reach that point, I wait, I save.  But most of the time I’m just struggling to make enough for the month.  Do I sometimes appear to make irrational or impatient purchases?  Perhaps to some extent, but nothing extreme.  And by the time I reach that point, I’ve usually already waited a year or two, just in case a second marshmallow appears and makes life easier.  But it’s one thing to wait a bit for a second marshmallow, and quite another to waste your life waiting on marshmallows.
