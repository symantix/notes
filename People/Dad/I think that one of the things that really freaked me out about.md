I think that one of the things that really freaked me out about pot when I was young, is that it helped me to understand many social nuances. But I didn't know what to do with the information. Do I really continue to go along with some of this horse shit, now that I understand it?  This pushed me a little futher in pro the mindset of an introvert. It's difficult to say how much trouble I had with social skills before pot, or how many other contributing factors there were, but suddenly understanding social nuances that I had never come close to understanding before, was a huge force in eventually and completely silencing me in social situations larger than three, sometimes even no larger than two people if I decided I had nothing in common with the second person. This is why I have always been lonely in bars. 

I was only in my early forties that I really began to understand that my knowledge of the bullshit was a tool, not a curse, and that strange people were not boring.  Quite the contrary, at the very least, they meant fresh new ideas.  All you need is the small courage it takes to engage, and a dab of wit and boldness when you want to bow out.
