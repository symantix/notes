For most of my adult life, I have found time to do the things that I want to do, by playing with the time that I have committed to others.  When I was younger, I simply didn’t justify this action.  Any moral ambiguity involved in my time manipulation, and any guilt it caused me, was simply set aside and ignored, until I simply couldn’t feel good about the volume of time I was misappropriating, at which point I would stop for a while.  As I got older, I actually did start justifying this habit by noting that I existed in a society which had no place for my real dreams without a tremendous exertion of time, effort, and money, one which I could never afford to make on my own, and that manipulating my time in a society that was constantly manipulating me to be someone that I’m not, was simply par for the course.

Now I’m in my forties, and I find that this skill that I have to manipulate time, this skill I’ve developed and honed to a sharp edge, is actually a great tool.  Now I use it to find time for all sorts of things, and not just for my own endeavors.  I find time to wash the dishes, or spend time with my family.  Now I’m even finding time to take care of stuff at my dumb job, that needs to be taken care of, but I would never be given time for by my boss.
