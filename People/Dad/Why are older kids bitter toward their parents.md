Why are older kids bitter toward their parents?  Because they think their parents don't like them.  Why would they think that?  Because their parents are always telling them to do something besides the thing they are already doing.  As kids get older, this becomes a real issue for them, because they want to become individuals, and we are still trying to mold them into who we think they should be.  These are the years when the definition of the parent's relationship to the child goes into flux.  As the child transistions into an adult, so should the parent transition into the all-accepting friend that they always hoped to be, slowly setting aside all notions of discipline and correction, and replacing them with respect and wisdom.
