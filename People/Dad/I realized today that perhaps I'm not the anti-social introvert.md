I realized today that perhaps I'm not the anti-social introvert that I once thought I was.  I love conversation.  I've always loved conversation.  I love it like I love art, which is the thing I love most in this world.  And, like art, I can grow sick of it, even quickly, and desire desperately to stop.  This is as true of conversation as it is of drawing.  It is these desperate moments that I'm thinking of, which happen often.  These are the moments that have caused me to judge myself so harshly, and perhaps incorrectly.  Are extroverts capable of simply pushing through these moments?  Do they even have them?  Or is this the definition of introvert, to quickly grow tired of social interaction?

The wonderful thing about getting sick of an art, is that there are several other art forms that are equally entertaining.  And so I have had long periods in my life when I simply haven't been interested in talking to people. Perhaps that's where the line is drawn.
