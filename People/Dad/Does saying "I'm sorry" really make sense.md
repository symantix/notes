Does saying "I'm sorry" really make sense?  Aren't we all human?  Don't we all make mistakes?  Should I be sorry, or just move on and trust my brain to learn?  If I fall while practicing a skateboard trick, should I feel guilty?  Should I apologize to the pavement, or maybe to the skateboard?  Or should I just get back up and try again?  

And before we go too far, is there a difference between trying to do good and making a mistake or a misstep, and doing something that you know might hurt an unwilling party?

Ultimately, I think some apologies are nothing more than social salves, which may or may not help the any victims, but is often harmful to the person making the mistake.  It teaches them to think of mistakes as something to regret, when they should be thinking of them as something to respect, appreciate, and reflect upon from time to time.  At the very least, there's a lot of great jokes buried in those memories.  

I think that perhaps, when we fumble, if there is a possibility that it may have hurt or frustrated someone around us, then perhaps a casual apology is wise, whether there is any moral obligation or not.  What's important, is that you don't confuse the relieving of others' emotional wounds, with any obligation for you to accept guilt or feel fault.  Peacemaking and guilt are not always intertwined.
