As I continue to try and commit to the work I love as if it were play, I find that at the beginning of each work session, I often don't feel like drawing, or writing, or doing anything.  I tell myself that I'll get into it once I sit down and try.  But it often feels miserable, and I fear that I'm going to give up again.

I think this might be the wrong approach.  I didn't just commit to doing the work I love on a regular basis; I committed to doing it as play.  I'm not supposed to feel like working.  So if I don't feel like drawing, then that's work.  I don't know how others define work, but to me work is clearly doing something that you don't want to do.  There is nothing wrong with working, and often it's necessary.  But if it makes you happy to do it, it's not work, it's play.

So if I don't want to write or draw, then I shouldn't.  I should play.  Maybe a game.  Perhaps a show on occasion, but preferably nothing that takes my mind too far away from my creative center.  I need to trust myself way more than I do.  Art and literature is play to me, I will come back to it naturally, and soon, if it's simply a whim, and not forced.

That's the theory anyway.

No matter what though, don't just sit there.  Do something.  Play is seldom idle or still.  Build or tear down.  Explore or create.  Solve a puzzle, or design one.  Never stop.
