My first exposure to adulthood, and the two decades to follow, was in a very religious region of the country.  The idea of fully expressing and locally publishing my innermost works of art never even crossed my mind.  I tend to think it was because I was so fearful of the idea of being surrounded by all the judgemental people, that my own mind simply protected me from any big idea that might draw the wrong kind of attention.  

And so I never bothered.

It's taken me this long to really face how often I have failed to be brave.

And so I'm trying harder.

I'm becoming more convinced that we all live in multiple dimensions simultaneously.  Because while I am very upset and hurt at the artistic loss of the past twenty years, I am also grateful for the loved ones it has brought my way.
