The reason why people get offended at nudity or violence in a movie, is because their moral framework dictates that they should actively protest such behavior, in this case by turning the show off.  But they can’t turn it off, because it’s so good.  So the movie has created a moral dilemma for them, which they then project onto the person who created it, who does not share the same moral framework.  They created a movie that overlaps the viewers interests, but not entirely, because the author is not a clone of the viewer.  You don’t see people asking why there was nudity in a porn, and that’s because the expectations could not be clearer with a porn, so people of a certain moral fabric reject it before they get mentally invested in it.

The problem is that the movie belongs to the author, not the viewer.  When you ask why there was nudity in your movie, you’re actually asking why there was nudity in the author’s movie, to which the answer is simple, because that’s what they wanted.
