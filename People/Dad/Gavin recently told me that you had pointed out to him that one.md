Gavin recently told me that you had pointed out to him that one day I could die of a heart attack.  I remember being in the studio when you said this to him.  I was sitting a distance away, remaining aloof to the conversation.  It didn’t bother me that you said that, nor does it now.  At the time, I felt that it was something he needed to hear.  But he explained to me that this is why he can’t stop telling me that he loves me over and over before he goes to bed, as he does his mom.  So much so that it is difficult to get him to go to bed.  He has this pattern as we are telling him to go to bed, of saying I love you so so much, and then when we say I love you to, he begins to talk to us about some random topic.  When we tell him to go to bed again, he says okay I love you so so much, and then when we say I love you too, he goes back into another random topic.  It was the day after I really took him to task about this one night, and ended up grounding him because he went through this loop so many times.  The next day he explained this to me.  I had to spend some time speaking to him about the realities of life, what a great privilege it was just to experience life, just to experience any of this.  I explained to him that this was his movie, and the he was the camera, the author, and the director, and how important it was that he work hard to write a good story for himself every day.
