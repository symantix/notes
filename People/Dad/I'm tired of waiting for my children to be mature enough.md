I'm tired of waiting for my children to be mature enough, responsible enough, kind enough, wtf ever, before I start being there real and close friend.  We love among many who argue that you can’t be a friend and a parent at the same time.  If my children don't encounter a strong and supportive voice in this house, they will seek one in the world around us, and they might not be as lucky as I was; the voice they find might lead them into dark places.

My dad turned my eyes from the sky, to him, if only for a few brief moments, in search for the divine. I'm not crazy enough to follow such notions, but the point is, he made a great and positive impact in my life, he taught me the martial arts of the spirit realm, and then he showed me that he was just another man like me.  He grew up in an oppressive home, in an oppressive society and limited options.  And then he escaped that society, their mindset, their mental illnesses, their box.

But he didn't do it alone, and he never asked me to step away and support him financially. That was 100% my choice.  When I suggested it, he strongly opposed it.  It was only years later, when I did I without asking him or discussing it with him, that he finally accepted my choice.

And sometimes I think that was a mistake.  And sometimes I don't. 

I think my dad's biggest mistake is that he tried to be my art director. I accepted it for a while, because he inspired me. But that was never a place I was going to remain, restricted by the ideas of another.  Not that I don't love collaborating, but with him it did, I hate to say it, sometimes feel a bit oppressive, since every day my art existed to please him.  Pleasing him was its meaning, its purpose.  So when he wasn’t pleased, the art, by definition, was a failure.  I had to change that.

On the other hand, I was at a place in my life at that time where, had I not had an art director, I might have given up the arts altogether and resigned my life to becoming a pipe fitter or welder.
