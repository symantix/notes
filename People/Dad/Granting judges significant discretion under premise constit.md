Granting judges significant discretion under the premise of constitutional indeterminacy introduces the potential for the judiciary to exert influence that may, over time, encroach upon or even undermine the authority of the other branches of government. If left unchecked, such expansive interpretive power could enable judges to shape or redefine fundamental legal and political principles, raising concerns that the judiciary might, in effect, subvert the constitutional order or threaten the separation of powers upon which republican governance is based. Accordingly, this is not an area in which ignorance or disengagement among the governed can be justified. An informed and vigilant citizenry is essential to ensuring that judicial power remains appropriately balanced and accountable within the constitutional framework, thereby safeguarding the integrity of democratic and republican institutions.
