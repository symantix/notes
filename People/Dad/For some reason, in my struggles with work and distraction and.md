For some reason, in my struggles with work and distraction and guilt, I keep forgetting one simple fact:  I can work harder at something I don’t like doing, if I spend a lot of time not doing it.  So I can sit and stare at my screen all day long, and try to stay focussed, and try to get a solid 8+ hours out of my fingertips, and I may get 8+ hours of work, but the work is going to look like what it was: weak and forced.  Or I can spend 1-2 hours doing something that is really personally fulfilling to me, and then 1-2 hours kicking ass with a solid focus and determination, and do the kind of work that I’m proud of, with the creative forces that make me who I am.  This approach, plus a great attitude toward my peers, is going to be more effective than a forced all-day approach anyway.

Stop feeling so guilty when you’re not sitting at your desk.  I think this problem is slowly fading away anyway, but it’s worth writing this down.
