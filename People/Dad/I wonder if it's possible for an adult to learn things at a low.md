I wonder if it's possible for an adult to learn things at a low level, to let go of our addiction to using the spoken word as our basis for thought, and instead, not to transcend, but to descend, and to think and learn with the parts of our brain that made learning how to use this new tool (the spoken language) possible in the first place.  Is it really reasonable to assume that we lose the ability to think in this way, to gain new tools in this way, after childhood?  What if we lose it, simply because it's a muscle that we stop flexing?  Can't we just, little by little, learn to start flexing those muscles again?

I sometimes feel that I am learning and doing things at this level already, certainly not with all things, but perhaps with some, and only sometimes.  Isn't this the place of zen that many have spent their whole life seeking?  Exploration and experimentation is in order.
