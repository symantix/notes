When I was in junior high school, a group of us were gathered together and presented with a challenge.  We were instructed to design a vehicle that could navigate a maze without being touched.  In hindsight, I’m convinced that we were being tested for cognition in the challenges of space travel.  

I think I failed the test.  I took the challenge on face value, that is to say any manner of physical contact.  With that in mind, I concluded that it was not possible with the tools that we were given, which included nothing electronic and nothing involving radio.  Some of my peers designed physical extensions made of wood and tape, which were used to steer their vehicles.  I had considered these options, but had decided that the vehicle was still being touched, just not directly by me.  I don’t remember if it was the kind of challenge that had a winner, but I did wonder if my interpretation of the rules had been too strict.
