Jim Henson died in 1990, when I was around 13.  About two years prior, my great grandmother passed away.  I have a memory from when I was a child, a memory from the moment when I learned that Jim Henson had passed. I was lying in bed weeping over the loss of him, and all that he might have created in the years to come.  That part of the memory is real.  But recently I realized that there is another aspect to that memory that couldn’t have happened, something impossible, something that had been altered at some point in time between then and now.  In the memory, I am lying in my great grandmother’s bed, a child no older than five.  When I wonder about when that part of the memory changed, I think that it must have been at the very beginning, in the moment when I lay there alone in my own bed, now an adolescent boy, whom, as everyone knows, should be tough, a young man who is clearly too old to cry.
