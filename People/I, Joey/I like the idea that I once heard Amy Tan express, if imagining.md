I like the idea that I once heard Amy Tan express, of imagining one’s ideal self, standing beside you at all times.  Perfectly compassionate, full of wisdom, with a complete understanding of what you’re capable of when you’re at your best.  It is there to advise at all times, in all places, or merely to sit together with you in silence.  Whatever it is that you need, it knows.  It’s not there to judge, only ever to help.  It is proud in your victories, and always has the right thing to say when you are struggling or defeated.  It is who I am, when I’m at my best.  Maybe it’s from the future, maybe it’s just my soul.  Either way, it’s up to me to imagine what that ideal self would say to me or others in any given moment, what it would do in my current situation.  And then it’s up to me to act as that person, rather than anything less.
