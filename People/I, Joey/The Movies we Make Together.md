Your eyes are cameras, and your brain is a recording device.  What you see around you is the movie, and your memory is the reruns.  The people around you are making their own movie, and you’re one of the stars. You’ll be in their reruns.  So, ask yourself what kind of movie you want a see, what kind of rerun you want to relive.  This movie isn’t just your own; this is just your version of the same movie everyone around you is recording.

“We have many judges, and that is God.”
~ Dr. Michael W. Jones

Our judges are all around us.  We are written in their heart, and on their mind.  We are also our own judge, as we watch our reruns.  It can be difficult when you’re young to understand just how much that’s going to mean, but it no fun to watch a really shitty movie over and over.  So if you’re not enjoying your movie at this point, it might be time to rethink some life choices!

Sometimes, when we are young, we don’t understand why certain choices are such terrible ideas, because we haven’t lived long enough to understand the universe around us more fully.  This is why it’s so important to trust our parents, elders, and leaders.  Their experience protects us from our own naïveté while our eyes are young.

But most importantly, at least in my life, are our children.  For now, we make a lot of decisions for them, we compel them into the routines that we believe will benefit them the most, we teach them what is right and wrong, and we decide if and how they should be disciplined if they cross the boundaries that we have drawn for them.  We do this because now we are the parent, now we are the elder, and they are the ones with young eyes.  But one day, they will be an adult, and then they will be an elder.  They will see the world more fully.  And then they will judge how we treated them when they were most vulnerable, and what we taught them when they were young.

There is a Judgment Day, and there is a God.  Not Jehovah, though he certainly had his day.  And not Jesus, though I have definitely witnessed his power.  It is us. It has always been us.  We will watch our movies, our reruns, and we will judge, and be judged, over and over and over again.
