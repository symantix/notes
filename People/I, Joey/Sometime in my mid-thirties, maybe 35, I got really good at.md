Sometime in my mid-thirties, maybe 35, I got really good at illustration.  I was always talented, but I wasn’t remotely satisfied with most of my work in my youth, and my illustration work still wasn’t consistently good enough for me in my early adult life.  But by 35, in spite of a sometimes smothering programming career, I had somehow honed my skills to the point where I could set my mind to drawing just about anything in a way that I found personally satisfying, and by forty I was convinced that most people would also be impressed.  And now, at 41, I know that my only weakness is my speed.  And so that’s what I’m most focused on now.
