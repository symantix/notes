**Nothing I Was Doing Was Wrong**

When I moved from working in my bedroom, to working next to my wife in the living room, I was initially stressed ... I eventually proceeded with a theory, that nothing I was doing was wrong.  This is a repeating pattern in my life, which I caught onto early on.  When I was very young, a babysitter scratched me on my back for not doing the dishes.  I lied to my mom and told her that I did do the dishes, and she easily found out the truth.  To my shock, she didn't care about anything I had done, her only concern was that the babysitter had hurt me, and that she had failed to protect me.  

It took me a while to fully reflect on the meaning of this moment, and other similar moments.  But at some point in my adult life, I decided that most of the things that I did in my life, acts that caused feelings of guilt, weren't actually wrong.  

I'm might sometimes annoying, or gross.  Sometimes I'm lazy, maybe even insufferable.  But I'm not a law-breaker, I'm not a criminal, nor am I even unfaithful, disloyal, unreliable, and certainly not abusive.
