Work is a habit.  It’s not something you force, though forcing it is part of how you form the habit.  Forcing it and not forcing it, giving yourself room to breath, but not enough room to give up.  It is a frame of mind, a way of living that is worth achieving.  Once you achieve it, it becomes less work, and more of a means to your end.  A tool, often fun, often fulfilling, sometimes frustrating and infuriating.  In all cases, it’s better than idleness, which is, truly, the tool of your animal nature.  Your mind will use idleness as a means to tear your own life apart.
