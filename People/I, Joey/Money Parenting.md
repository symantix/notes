Money Parenting

I use money to motivate my children toward anything that they seem to find difficult.  Grades, chores, behavior, etc.  I haven’t told them this overtly, for fear that they might pretend to find things difficult just to make a profit.  But when I see them struggling, among the first of my ideas is usually money.  Spending time with them?  Yes.  Working with them and helping them?  Absolutely.  But when all else fails, money is the universal motivator, because it is not anything, and yet it is everything.  It is the universal symbol of our dreams and aspirations.  It can be ugly, but it can also be beautiful.  Money is, quite literally, what we make it.  And so it can spark parts of our brains that work by faith, because money is faith in the future.  It can turn lights on in our minds, because now we know, deep inside, that the energy we are using won’t go to waste.  Is there anything nobler than money?  Yes, and money can often help us to achieve many of those things.  Is there any better motivator?  Yes, many.  But there is perhaps no motivating idea easier to grasp, than money.
