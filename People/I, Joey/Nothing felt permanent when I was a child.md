Nothing felt permanent when I was a child.  My first memories were of living with my Great-Grandmother.  I remember my Mom calling and saying that she was going to come get me one day, and then one day, when I was almost 5, she did.  When I was 17, I asked my Mom’s blessing to move out, promising that I would stay in school.  When she denied her blessing, I moved out anyway, and when the school year started, I didn’t attend.  When I came back home a year later, my Mom gave me enough time to get my GED, and then she asked me to leave, helping me get a job in east Texas with some distant relatives.  It was there that I eventually came to live with Dr. Jones’ family.  He has spent a lot of time with me since that time in my early adult life, teaching me about family, permanence, and life-long commitments.  It is a concept that my brain has battled over the years.  Without him, I’m not sure anyone would have had a permanent place in my life.
