### Me

It’s a valid point, for sure.  But I see both views as having some truth, and perhaps balancing each other in a way.  On the one hand, there are irresponsible decisions that can make us happy, that we simply shouldn’t make.  These decisions run the gamut, from driving twice the speed limit, to subjugating other people for our benefit.  

On the other hand, there are decisions like, who you are, what defines you, and what you want to do with your life.  Unfortunately, people who are not you sometimes like to get upset about very personal decisions like this, when it’s none of their business.  And so we must be willing to let those people be unhappy without it making us unhappy.  

And then there are decisions in between, like driving 65 in a 55 zone, putting your bag in a seat on a bus that’s 75% full, riding your bicycle on the sidewalk, or farting in a public place.  At the end of the day, you’ve gotta decide where you stand on all these issues, and let go of how it makes anyone else feel.

This is a very broad statement, and we all make broad statements all the time.  The only way to know what is actually meant, is to know something about the person who is saying it.
