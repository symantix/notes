When I grow up, I want to be undeniable.

When I was a kid, I spent some time studying kids who seemed to be leaders.  I asked myself why others followed them. I concluded that it was because they had a cool idea.  Whether it be fashion, attitude, skill, these were all ideas.  Some kids followed the ideas, others came up with the ideas.  I also concluded something that turned out to be false, but it was useful to has believed it. I concluded that what also made them a leader, was that they didn’t care what others thought, they just did what they wanted.  But doing what you wanted wasn’t enough, because there were many who did that, and they were alone.  The key, I concluded, was to be undeniable.  So I brought substance to my ideas, fleshed them out myself, and kept my eyes forward, not worrying about whether my ideas met the approval of others.  The question was simple:  was I enjoying myself, or not?  And this approach to life, did slowly yield the results I expected.  A few people began to join my groups, and do my thing.  Some of those people have become life-long friends.
