Lot's of book burning and statue toppling going on today, as our culture decides what we want to celebrate, what we want to denounce, what we wish to remember, and what we wish to forget.  

It's actually terrible, because what we should remember is everything.  Selective memory is a great recipe for repeating the mistakes of the past.

As we go through these perilous times, maybe we should all preserve the ideas that mean the most to us, and pass them on to our descendants.  I, for one, hope that we never forget the ideas that Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. put forth in his incredible speech.  For so long, many of us clung to these ideas, and longed for the day when they would be realized by all.  Now, it seems like some these ideas are fearcely hated by many who would claim to be the least racist among us.
