Frustrated because I have too much paid work and want to do something else.  Stressed when I’m doing something else because I’ve gotta make some money or keep some clients happy.  This is one of my default positions.  I need to find the path to get as far away from this place as possible...

Sitting here a couple of hours after writing the above, now in a different frame of mind.  Things look different from here.  When I take a break, I do so cautiously.  What’s wrong with that?  I remember an ancient story where God blessed the warriors who kept their eyes on the landscape as they drank from the river.  Perhaps the impulses initially described are just me going too far, i.e. caution is warranted, but frustration and stress is not.  The greatest acrobat is the one who is not fearful because he knows that he is attentive, and he trusts his own decisions, literally with his very life.  It’s worth taking the time to go through a mental checklist, to consider the consequences of our choices.  Not fearfully, but prudently.  We must be motivated and ready to face the truth, even if it means break time is over.  It is always a happy moment when we see the storm coming from far away, and begin to make preparations with an ease of confidence, knowing that there is time, and knowing that we are capable to the task.
