I think that when two married couples become friends, that it’s important that the spouses with the same gender preference form the strongest friendship.  I enjoy thinking of it this way, because I remember when Shawn’s sister and her spouse were in town, and we had them over for a night on the porch full of conversation and music.  I connected easily with Shawn’s sister, grey, who was clearly the more masculine of the two.  She was also attractive, as a woman, in her own way.  But I felt absolutely no sexual tension or awkwardness.  It felt very much like befriending a man.  Her wife was also really nice, and I admired their relationship, and welcomed her as a friend.  But the bond of friendship wasn’t forming as fast with her, nor was I focusing on it as much.

We now have two new married couples coming to the studio, and I am taking a similar approach with them, focussing on the men, while being welcoming to the women, and allowing them to spend time with the other women.  This feels like the most solid approach to creating platonic adult relationships that are free of poisonous sexual complexities and entanglements.
