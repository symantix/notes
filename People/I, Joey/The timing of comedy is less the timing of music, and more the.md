The timing of comedy is less the timing of music, and more the timing of conversation.  It is less ryhthmic, more natural, like the wind in the branches.  It is the timing of a person who proposes marriage to their best friend, or the woman who tells her spouse that she's pregnant, or the man who tells his family that his days on earth are numbered.  It is the setting of the scene, it is the tone of nature in you, speaking to the nature of the audience, a sub-verbal signal that meaningful communication is in progress, a broad engagement of the mind, and delivery of the package you've just unwrapped for them.  The quality of the timing, and the quality of a joke like this, shouldn't be measured according to the quality of its verbal structure.  It's more primal, and it should simply be measured by the delight that is created throughout the life of the joke.
