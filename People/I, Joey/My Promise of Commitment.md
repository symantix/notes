My Promise of Commitment

I, Joey, do recognize that within the center of my very being, there is a spirit who wants to thrive, who wants to achieve, and fulfill the very best of my dreams.  I do also recognize that there is another spirit, an ancient force of nature, that seeks pleasure before pain, and doesn’t understand the need for suffering and sacrifice, and the true cost of finding fulfillment for myself and others.  

I also recognize that we all come from the Earth, and to the Earth we will return.  I am a part of this world, and everything, and everyone in it.  We are all connected, and together we can accomplish what would be impossible alone.  If I am to ever sort out and overcome the battles that wage inside me, or understand the world around me, I must never forget that.

Therefore, from this day forward, I commit myself to the council of my elders, those who have gone through the trials of life and witnessed the nature of these battles that wage within and around us; and to the council of my advisors, those who fight by my side in this brave new world.  I will listen to their words, and consider them carefully before setting them down or adopting them as my own.  

~~And on this day, I commit myself to the discipline of sociology, for the rest of my existence in this body.  I pledge to submit myself to the instruction of my teachers, and explore this discipline at the standard and curriculum they set out for me.~~  I promise that I will not give up, and that I will not give in to the spirits who oppose self sacrifice, to the voices that deceive me into thinking that play is more important than work.  I promise to heed the guidance of my teachers in these matters, and stay the course to which I have committed until it is complete.  I promise to value skill over pleasure, achievement over entertainment, and work over play.  And I promise to remain forever connected to those elders, those advisors, and those teachers who keep me well grounded in this present world.

I pledge these things by the power of the community, the power of the family, and the power within me from the day of my conception.

Amen
