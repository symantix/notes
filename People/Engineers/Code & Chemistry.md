Experienced developers are relatively immune to simple mistakes and syntax errors.  They happen, but with the utilization of developer consoles, network and compile monitors, and other tools, most basic errors are flagged before they ever leave a local test environment.  The mistakes that we are most vulnerable to are the unforeseen possibilities that can occur when data interacts with code.  In the professional realm, bugs usually occur when a developer fails to foresee and account for all of the possible outcomes.  It’s analogous to chemistry, in which the most important task is to minimize the inevitable damage that will occur when new formulas are exposed to a population, knowing that no amount of particle simulation can produce the magnitude of chaos that an application needs to become stable.
