The look on my daughter’s face

- When she came to our party with two broken arms, before she lived with us.  Her smile beamed, and she drew every other child to her.
- When she first saw Hambone, our American Bully.  She had just woken up, and she knew that she was going to meet a new dog that she did not choose.  When she saw him, she tried so hard to bury her growing smile.
- When she gets my subtle humor.  Like when we’re watching The Kardashians together, her show, and we suddenly had to leave.  She left the show on the screen as we got up, and without any clue in tone or tenor, I said, “It’s a good thing we can pause this.”  The memory of her slowly turning head brings me no end of delight.
