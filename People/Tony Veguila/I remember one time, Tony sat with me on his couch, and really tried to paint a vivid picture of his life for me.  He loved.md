I remember one time, Tony sat with me on his couch, and really tried to paint a vivid picture of his life for me.  He loved partying and just being generally irresponsible.  I thought he was so cool and independent, and I admired that he could pay for his own place with his job at Sonic, and be free to make those choices to just do whatever he wanted.  He knew that I was considering a similar path, so one time when we were hanging out, he took the time to paint a detailed picture of how much he struggled every day to survive.  He completely abandoned any desire he may have had to maintain his cool visade in front of me, and described himself as a poor, struggling young adult who was either paying the price for some terrible life decisions, or was in this situation because life had simply dealt him a bad hand (I don’t recall which it was).  When my mom called his house, he wanted me to speak to her, but I refused.  When she took my car, if I remember correctly, he was pretty amused.
