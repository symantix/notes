Understanding a problem is entirely different from achieving an effective solution.

Capitalism certainly has its millionaires and billionaires, and I’m happy for them.  Capitalism has also pulled far more people out of poverty than communism ever has, on a large scale, in practice.

That said, I think we agree on this:  We can do far better.  Some ideas of communism and socialism work well on a small scale, as you have pointed out.  That’s why these ideas work well in churches and communities.  But if history has taught us anything, we should be very careful about scaling that up, and putting the responsibility of effective socialism in the hands of people in far away places, who don’t know us, and care far less about us than they do their own community, or worse.  Leave that job, and those resources, to the community leaders.  

As for the means of production:  Nothing yet has proven to work better than self-organization, and that’s why I favor the free market.

As for doing better:  That’s why we both remain willing to engage in civil discourse.  This remains our nation’s greatest strength, and the primary reason why we have come so far in so little time.
