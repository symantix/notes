Yes, Adolf Hitler and Nazi propaganda frequently referred to Jews as "vermin" or similar dehumanizing terms like "parasites" and "rats." This language was used to vilify and dehumanize Jewish people, portraying them as a threat to society to justify discrimination, persecution, and the Holocaust. Such rhetoric was common in Nazi speeches, writings, and media, including in Hitler's book Mein Kampf and in propaganda materials like the film The Eternal Jew (1940), which explicitly compared Jews to vermin.
