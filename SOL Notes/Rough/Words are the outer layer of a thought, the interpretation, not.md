- Words are the outer layer of a thought, the interpretation, not the thought itself.  Thoughts are like images, but they are not images, they are something unlike anything else.  This may be why we added “idea”, to the definition of a noun, because a thought is neither a person, place, nor a thing, nor is it the sensor data that we receive from such as these. Words are merely one of a virtually unlimited way that we can grasp a thought, as there are a virtually unlimited number of ways to grasp a thought with words.  And yet some thoughts are completely ungraspable, and they are gone as quickly as they came.
