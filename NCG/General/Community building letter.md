Dear Community Member,

This Tuesday, August 20, at 7pm, we will be gathering for “Building Meaningful Community”, a new kind of neighborhood watch meeting.  You are humbly invited to attend.

Our neighborhood is diverse, we all interpret things differently, and it can sometimes take a great deal of patience to understand our fellow man.  It’s far easier to allow injustice to fall upon those whom we don’t know, and it is likewise easier to commit crimes upon those whom we think of as enemies.  We witness this in its extreme during times of war, but it can also fester and grow in our communities when we refuse to connect to our neighbors.  Let us seek the good in others by showing them the goodness in ourselves, and by doing so we will dispel all fictional notions birthed from ignorance, connect to each other based on the truth of who we really are, and poison the roots of darkness that allow crime to exist and grow.

The best way to watch a community, is to know the community.  And the best way to know the community, is to build the community and care for the community.  This requires that we participate functionally and actively in group discussions, and invest our lives in reaching out and helping the community around us in meaningful ways.

This community will continue to work with law enforcement, whom we expect to dedicate themselves to the service and well-being of this community and all of its members.  We will maintain open channels of communication with our government employees, so that they can understand how to carry out their purpose most effectively and compassionately.

So mark your calendars:

Time: This Tuesday, August 20th, at 7pm
Place: Revival Center on Bainbridge

I’m always available for questions, thoughts, and discussions.

Sincerely,

Michael Jones
