Pan seared beet fries.

Pre-roast beets for 20-25 minutes on 400, or pressure cook on high for 12-13 minutes.  Raw beets are very tough, so they need to be pre-tenderized.
Allow the beets to cool for 15-20 minutes, then rub their skin off with a paper towel.  If you've cooked them long enough, this step is very easy.
Cut beets into 1/4" thick slices, then cut the slices into 1/4" thick fries.
Pro tip:  Do this with lots of beets, allow them to cool down completely, then store the pre-roasted fries in a sealed container in the fridge.  Now you can have fries for days with minimal work.

Pan on high, pre-heated.  The larger the pan, the better.  Non-stick or cast iron is the only way to go here.
Add grape seed oil to the hot pan, and allow a minute for the oil to heat up.  Warning:  Only use high-temp oils like grape seed for this recipe.
Carefully fill pan with a single layer of fries.
Add salt while cooking.  I have a 12" pan, and sprinkle 1 big 3-finger pinch across all the fries at this stage.  This allows the salt to cook into the vegetables and infuse them with savory.
Cook on high temp for 4-5 minutes, uncovered.
Give the fries a few tosses, to flip as many fries as possible.  If you're not comfortable with a toss, you can use a spatula.  They don't all have to be flipped, just do your best and flip most of them.

Consider adding another big pinch of salt at this point.  The key to making these fries delicious, is balancing the natural mild sweetness of the beet, with a savory kick from the salt.  
If you have blood pressure issues, consider this:  beets are probably the best vegetable for the cardiovascular system, so if you eat a lot of them, you can probably use a bit more salt without worrying about it.  
So don't go crazy, just monitor your bp, and make sure your is food delicious.

Cook the fries for another 4-5 minutes.

At this point, your fries should be browning very nicely.  Some of them may be black.  These are still good, so enjoy, and keep practicing your timing when cooking these, so they are cooked exactly as you like them.  For me, I like a bit of black charring, it adds a nice flavor element.  They also need to be fork-tender.  So poke 2-3 of them throughout the pan, and taste 1-2 samples.  They may need another minute or two.  This part gets better with practice, but they're gonna be great no matter what.

With a large slotted spoon, scoop your fries into a large plate with 1-2 layers of paper towels, to absorb any excess oil.

While the fries are still really hot and oily, add your favorite salt-free herb blend.  I love garlic and herb.  I'm also going to try a chipotle blend soon.  Add a bunch of it, just make sure it's salt free, so that you can control your salt levels during the cook, without any risk of over-salting later.
