My wife never asked me to lift a finger for ten years of our marriage.  In the rarest of moments when she did, I hopped to, and I always encouraged her to ask me anytime she wanted me to help out.  But she just wouldn't.  Sometimes I'd find her in a situation that one person shouldn't be in by themselves, and I would be aggravated that she didn't say something.  But she just wouldn't.  I couldn't understand it.

Then one day, years into our marriage, I started washing the dishes on a regular basis.  No one asked me, I just did it.  When she noticed, I'm not sure I'd ever seen her quite that excited  before.  She gave me a huge hug from behind, couldn't keep her hands off me later.

After about a month of this, I was sitting on my back porch, painting, as we failures so often do.  She came out on the porch, and I asked her if she would make me a sandwich.  And do you know that woman glared at me?  I laughed, told her I was joking, and got up and made my own goddamn sandwich.
