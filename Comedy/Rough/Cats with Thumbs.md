I’ve been thinking a lot about my worst fears.  For the first thirty years of my life, I’m not sure that I ever feared anything.  I would go into risky situations thinking, why not?  What is there to fear?  I was smart, and I knew how far was too far.  So I went snowboarding and rock climbing, water skiing, fishing, and turkey hunting.  I was homeless, but I wasn’t scared.  I was hungry and miserable sometimes. That time didn’t last long, or I’m sure it would’ve eventually gotten to me.  I went hitchhiking across Texas.  I’m lucky I survived either of these experiences, in retrospect.  I rode in a hot air balloon with my wife.  I’ve rode jet skis with my children.  I’ve paddleboarded far out into open sea, where other sound from man can be heard, and swam with sharks, dolphins, and manta rays.  And who knows what the future holds.  I’ve always felt that grave misfortune is unlikely, and mild misfortune is manageable.
