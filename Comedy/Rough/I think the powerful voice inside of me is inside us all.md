I think the powerful voice inside of me is inside us all.  The problem is our memory.  If a thought isn't connected to an event, it almost certainly will be forgotten.  A thought never existed if it happened in a void.  So we go through our whole life, having poignant thoughts, but forgetting about them, never acting on them, and never remembering that we've had a million of them before.  Eventually that voice, strong in meaning as it may be, fades away in volume, to the point where we barely notice that it's there anymore.  The only difference between me and these others, is that I made a pact with myself not long ago, to stop what I'm doing if **at all** possible, and write down those thoughts when I hear them.  So now those thoughts are connected to events, which connects them to memory.  And now the feeling of those thoughts has become so familiar, that I now remember having that feeling many times before, when I was a younger man, when I was a teen, and when I was a child.  I don't remember the content of those meaningful thoughts, only that I had them.
