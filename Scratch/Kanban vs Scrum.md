Kanban vs Scrum 

I think the only thing we should take from Scrum is the idea of shooting for a deadline.  Don’t need:

- Releases at end of sprint (though this could be beneficial if everyone commits to testing)
- No changes during sprint (hot fixes or even full commits could be allowed)
- What happens if we over-estimate the work, and have time at end of sprint?  This is where above flexibility comes into play 
- We should consider and eow meeting, to report and put a cap on weekly production
- Failure to achieve a sprint is not necessarilya sign of slackness, particularly early on when we are trying to understand our average velocity.  That said, competing against our own velocity scores could be beneficial.
- I need to collect weekly estimates from devs on hours they can commit for the week.
