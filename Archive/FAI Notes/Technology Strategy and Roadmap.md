Technology Strategy and Roadmap

Let’s focus on our strengths.  We have a team of talented policy writers, and a network of top minds throughout the political spectrum.  Focusing on our content is the best place to begin.  Our website is a good start for content delivery.  

When I was promoted to Director of Engineering, I took it upon myself to begin looking around at our team and our tech projects.  While most of our projects were dead, dormant, or well attended to, I. Saw that perhaps our most important project was being neglected. After completing our main website. The engineering work that was put towards it was basically just putting out fires.

We need to stabilize it, organize it, fill in any placeholders (such as empty bios), strip off anything that isn’t growing or serving our core goals, and optimize the application for discoverability and readability.  These things, and maybe a few custom illustrations, will help to set it apart as a contender among major media outlets.

The measure of achieving this milestone should the response of our network.  How many views we’re getting, what anonymous visitors are saying, none of this is relevant at this point.  We need to posture ourselves for millions of viewers, and that begins by listening to the smartest minds that we know.

In the interim, we should start focusing more on regular posting to mute major social media outlets.  I see some posting happening, but we should be posting something at least 1-2 times per day, to start.  Is we don’t have any major articles ready to publish, we can focus on news or opinion pieces.

Once this milestone is achieved, we should start thinking about interactivity. I don’t think our application supports membership or commenting.  Comment sections can be a hairball if not managed properly.  I’d like to propose a way of organizing comments that has never been done before, to my knowledge.
